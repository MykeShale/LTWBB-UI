Today, LTWBB has supported over 2,000 documented cases and reached more than 20,000 youth.

> In July 2025, Rhoda was appointed Private Secretary and Senior Office Administrator to the Deputy Governor of Murang'a County -at age 24. This dual positioning -grassroots advocate and senior government official -gives LTWBB a unique ability to influence both community culture and institutional policy around mental health. She is currently pursuing a Master's degree in International Relations and Diplomacy at Mount Kenya University.

## SECTION 4: OUR STORY TIMELINE (Expanded)

**Eyebrow:** `OUR JOURNEY`

**Headline:**
> From a Conversation to a Movement

**6 Milestones:**

**2021 -The Beginning**
> Founded in November 2021 as a peer-to-peer mental health initiative at Murang'a University of Technology. Rhoda, then 21, turned peer counseling conversations into a structured advocacy effort. The name "Bossbaby" was chosen deliberately -authority and accessibility, in one brand.

**2022 -First Recognition**
> LTWBB received its first national recognition: Activist of the Year 2022. Campus outreach programs expanded. The first awareness walks were organized in Murang'a County, drawing students, community leaders, and local media.

**2023 -Building the Foundation**
> Over 570 mental health cases were documented and supported while Rhoda was still a full-time student. She completed the Nivishe Foundation 12-week Mental Health Advocacy Fellowship, becoming a Certified Mental Health Advocate. LTWBB's nature-based therapy programs launched -Tree Therapy and Counseling in Nature.

**2024 -Official Registration**
> LTWBB was formally registered as a Community Based Organization (CBO) in 2024, enabling institutional partnerships, official fundraising, and government collaboration. The Green Schools Program launched, reaching 15 educational institutions. Over 2,000 cumulative cases supported.

**2025 -Governance & Scale**
> Rhoda was appointed Private Secretary to the Deputy Governor of Murang'a County -at 24. LTWBB joined the Murang'a County Gender Technical Working Group as Liaison Person. AgriTech partnership launched at the 8th Africa Agri Expo, KICC Nairobi. Civil Servants Program expanded to 8 institutions.

**2026 -National Recognition & Global Vision**
> Young Advocate for Mental Wellness 2026 award. Over 20,000 youth reached. LTWBB website launched. International partnerships initiated. The movement that began as one conversation is now shaping Kenya's mental health policy landscape.

### PROGRAM 1: Counseling in Nature
**Category:** Individual · Therapeutic Services
**Target:** Adults, students, professionals

**Full description:**
> LTWBB's Counseling in Nature program brings professional therapeutic sessions into natural outdoor settings -forests, parks, riverbanks, and open landscapes across Kenya. Research consistently demonstrates that nature exposure reduces cortisol levels, lowers anxiety, and enhances the quality of therapeutic conversations.
>
> Sessions are facilitated by certified counselors trained in both evidence-based therapeutic approaches and nature-based intervention techniques. Sessions are one-on-one or group-based, depending on need. Programs are available in Murang'a County, with virtual extensions for remote participants.

**Who it's for:** Individuals experiencing anxiety, depression, grief, burnout, or relationship challenges who prefer a non-clinical setting.

**Impact:**
- 200+ sessions delivered
- 95% participant satisfaction rate
- Available in English and Swahili

**CTA:** [Book a Nature Counseling Session →]

### PROGRAM 2: Tree Therapy Project
**Category:** Community · Environmental Healing
**Target:** Community groups, individuals

**Full description:**
> The Tree Therapy Project is LTWBB's flagship program -a pioneering approach that integrates mental wellness with environmental stewardship. Participants come together to plant trees while engaging in guided therapeutic conversations, peer support, and mindfulness exercises.

> The act of planting -of nurturing something living -is itself therapeutic. Participants report a sense of purpose, connection, and hope from the experience. The trees planted are a permanent, visible legacy of the healing that took place.
>
> The program runs in community spaces, schools, and nature reserves across Murang'a County and beyond.

**Who it's for:** Community members, students, and groups seeking healing through collective action.

**Impact:**
- 2,500+ trees planted
- 300+ participants
- Operating since 2021
